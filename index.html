Agile perceptive multi-skill locomotion for quadrupedal robots in the wild

Enabling quadrupedal robots to traverse complex terrains—from rugged outdoor environments to urban landscapes—requires seamless integration of multiple motor skills, smooth transitions between gaits, and high-speed perceptive locomotion using only onboard sensors.


We present APT-RL (Action Pretrained Transformer-based Reinforcement Learning), a unified framework that enables multi-skill locomotion to achieve high-speed traversal in complex environments through autonomous skill transitions utilizing only onboard perception and computation.


Real-world experiments demonstrate that KAIST HOUND performs agile maneuvers through complex indoor obstacles and outdoor wild environments, reaching instantaneous peak speeds of up to 6 m/s while traversing stairs, hurdles, stepping stones, gaps, and fallen branches.

APT-RL Pipeline

Our approach generates large-scale, feature-rich 2D motion datasets through trajectory optimization with simplified dynamics (Single Rigid Body Dynamics). This produces 180,000 trajectories spanning 15.5 hours of motion data in just 8 minutes.

APT-RL consists of three integrated phases:

  1. Representation Learning — A Transformer-based VAE (TVAE) learns a unified latent space from TO-generated trotting and bounding motion data, with gait-specific torque decoders trained via supervised learning.
  2. Reinforcement Learning — A policy learns to select latent actions decoded into joint torques, combined with an auxiliary action for adaptation to complex 3D terrains unseen during pretraining.
  3. Perceptual Distillation — A student encoder trained on depth camera images and 2D LiDAR data mimics a privileged teacher encoder, enabling zero-shot sim-to-real deployment.

Our controller enables KAIST HOUND to autonomously select between trotting and bounding gaits based on terrain geometry, proprioceptive state, and commanded velocity — achieving agile traversal across unstructured outdoor environments.

Adaptive Skill Selection in the Wild

In a 1.1 km urban campus traversal, the robot adapted its gait in response to terrain characteristics and commanded velocity — using trotting at lower speeds and bounding to descend stairs at 4.3 m/s, reaching instantaneous peak speeds of up to 6 m/s.

In a 0.34 km wild forest traversal, the robot autonomously selected between trotting and bounding to navigate fallen trees, exposed roots, uneven logs, and slippery leaf-covered ground using only onboard LiDAR and a depth camera.

From 2D Trajectories to 3D Agile Locomotion

Our key insight lies in generating large-scale, feature-rich 2D motion datasets through trajectory optimization with simplified dynamics (SRBD), while keeping data generation affordable — 180,000 trajectories in just 8 minutes.

Representation Learning A Transformer-based VAE encodes trotting and bounding motion sequences into a unified latent space. Gait-specific torque decoders are trained via supervised learning, acquiring reusable action priors without any RL-based representation learning or online trajectory optimization.

Auxiliary Action The RL policy jointly learns a latent action (decoded to torques via the pretrained decoder) and an auxiliary action (applied through a PD controller). This enables the policy to generate novel 3D motions beyond the coverage of the 2D pretraining dataset — including dynamic jumping, leg-fracture recovery, and in-place yaw rotation.

KAIST HOUND

We deploy APT-RL on KAIST HOUND, an in-house quadruped robot designed for fast and efficient locomotion. The robot is equipped with a D435 depth camera and a 2D LiDAR sensor for onboard perception, running all computation onboard.

To handle high-impact locomotion at speeds exceeding 4 m/s, we designed custom 3D-printed mechanical vibration absorbers mounted between the robot head and the LiDAR sensor, reducing impact loads that would otherwise exceed 10 g and cause sensor malfunctions.
